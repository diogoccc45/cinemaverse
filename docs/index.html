what it does

A data science project
about cinema.

Built from a raw Letterboxd export, enriched with TMDb metadata, and analysed through the lens of network science, ML and NLP.

◈
Exploration
Genre profiles, director bubbles, actor rankings, production company analysis and me vs the crowd.
⬡
Network Science
Film graph built from shared actors and directors. Louvain community detection. Comparison with Erdős–Rényi, Barabási–Albert and Watts–Strogatz models.
✦
Film Galaxy
Every film projected into 2D via UMAP + HDBSCAN clustering. Coloured by rating, cluster or decade. Hover for details.
→
Recommendations
Cosine similarity and semantic embeddings via sentence-transformers to rank my watchlist and surface new discoveries.
◎
Rating Prediction
Random Forest trained on my ratings with weighted director, genre and franchise features. Solves cold-start via synopsis embeddings.
✎
AI Reviews
DistilGPT-2 fine-tuned on 417 of my Letterboxd reviews. Generates new reviews in my writing style — with honest caveats about its limitations.
◉
Director Deep-Dive
Per-director profile with photo, bio, film grid, rating timeline, word cloud of my reviews and head-to-head comparison between directors.
▤
Lists
My curated Letterboxd lists with TMDb poster art, ratings and full reviews accessible in a scrollable grid.

network science

My films form
a small world.

Every film I've watched is a node. Two films connect when they share a director or actor. The result is a network — and it reveals something surprising about my taste.

what is a network?
Each film is a node. Two films connect when they share a director or actor — like Inception and The Dark Knight both directed by Nolan. I built this network across all 778 films I've watched.
small-world sigma
13.5
THRESHOLD
1.0
Sigma > 1 means small-world. Mine is 13.5× above the threshold — exceptionally high. Most real networks score between 1 and 5.
clustering
0.776
Films I watch tend to cluster tightly — if I like director A and director B, they likely worked with the same actors.
avg path length
3.69
Any two films in my history connect through just 3.7 hops. Cinema's version of six degrees of separation.
verdict
My taste network is a strong small-world. Highly clustered like Watts-Strogatz, but with the long-range connections of Barabási-Albert. My watching habits are neither random nor purely tribal — they form a rich, interconnected web.
